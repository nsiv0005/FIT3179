Australian Migration

Monitoring the rise and trends in Australian Migration

Trends in migration in and out of Australia

This graph illustrates the changes and population sizes of migrants coming into and leaving Australia. Although the graph shows significant range from over 1,000,000 to -15,000, the top few countries make up the bulk of Australian migration with the UK consistantly having over 1,000,000 migrants, and both India and China having increasing populations up to 1,000,000. The ultimate trend is a consistant rise in migration to Austrlia.

Most popular Australian Migrant Locations

This dataset reflects migrant resident locations in Australia for 2022 and 2023. Regions like Melbourne City and Sydney Inner City see the highest levels of net overseas migration which as much as 25,000 people residing there compared to the average location migrant population of 2,693, suggesting they are major hubs for international arrivals. The wide variability indicates that while some areas attract large numbers of migrants, others see much lower migration, which may be influenced by factors such as job opportunities, urbanization, and infrastructure.

Migrant Population ages coming to Australia

The data reveals trends in the composition of Australia's population, comparing Australian-born and overseas-born individuals across different age groups. Younger age groups, particularly those under 24, have a significantly higher proportion of Australian-born individuals, while the percentage of overseas-born individuals increases in the older age groups. This suggests that immigration to Australia is more prevalent among adults aged 24-45, potentially reflecting patterns where immigrants move to Australia for work, family or higher educational reasons. The growing proportion of overseas-born individuals in the working-age groups also indicates that Australia remains an attractive destination for skilled workers and professionals.

Second last graph

The estimated resident population data by country of birth shows significant growth between 2013 and 2023. In 2013, the highest population was from England with 1,012,780, while by 2023, this number had decreased to 961,570. Countries like India saw substantial growth, rising from 378,480 in 2013 to 845,800 in 2023. On average, the populations increased over time, with the mean value rising from 399,130 in 2018 to 445,530 in 2023, indicating Australia's continued attraction as a destination for migrants from diverse backgrounds.

Graph 5

The dataset below shows migration to and from Australia as well as net Australian migration. It indicates that up until mid-2019, net Migration was consistantly up so more people were constantly flowing into Australia reaching a max of 618,360 migrant arrivals. In 2020 however, this took a massive dip with migrant arrivals dropping to 113,000 as well as migrant depatures dropping which resulted in a net negative migration to -94,340. This shows the effect that COVID had on Australian migration which halted migrant arrivals. However in the consequent years we can see a sharp increase in migrant arrivals which has again boosted net migration indicating global population shifts post COVID.
